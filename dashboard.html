Reading Patterns & Tech Signals

Big caveat first. Chart patterns are probabilistic — one input among many. They are deterministic geometric reads of recent price pivots, not predictions. Use them alongside fundamentals, valuation, notes, and the assessment rationale. All signals are computed from ~2 years of daily history using an adaptive, volatility-scaled zig-zag.

1 · Status: forming vs confirmed

StatusMeaning
formingThe shape is in place, but price has not yet broken the key level in the pattern's direction. A watch item, not a trigger.
confirmedPrice has broken through the key level (neckline / support / resistance) in the pattern's direction. The measured-move target becomes active.

Example: a Double Top is forming while price is above its neckline, and only confirmed once price closes below it — which is why a stock near its highs can show "Double Top · forming" without it being a sell signal yet.

Badge codes: H&S Head & Shoulders · iH&S Inverse H&S · DT Double Top · DB Double Bottom · Asc△ Ascending · Desc△ Descending · Sym△ Symmetrical Triangle. Hover any badge for full name, direction, status, and confidence.

4 · Key level (Neckline) & Target

Key level — the line price must break to confirm: neckline (doubles, H&S), resistance/support (triangles), or apex (symmetrical). Shown in the Neckline column.

Target — the projected move after a confirmed break, equal to the pattern's height measured from the key level (green for bullish, red for bearish). Triangles report no target.

5 · The patterns

PatternShape · confirms when · reads as
Double TopTop → Neckline (trough) → Top at similar levels. Confirms below the neckline. Failed retest of resistance.
Double BottomBottom → Neckline (peak) → Bottom at similar levels. Confirms above the neckline. Support holding twice.
Head & ShouldersLS → Head (higher) → RS. Neckline = avg of the two troughs. Confirms below. Classic topping structure.
Inverse H&SThe mirror — LS → Head (lower) → RS. Confirms above the neckline. Classic bottoming structure.
Ascending △Flat highs (≥3 touches) + rising lows. Confirms above the highs. Breakout bias.
Descending △Flat lows (≥3 touches) + falling highs. Confirms below the lows. Breakdown bias.
Symmetrical △Falling highs and rising lows converging. Always forming until it breaks; watch the direction.

Only one pattern per symbol is shown — the most structurally complete read (H&S > triangles > doubles).

6 · Trends (the legs)

The Trends column counts the dominant zig-zag legs over the adaptive window, e.g. 5 Legs (3 ↑, 2 ↓). On the Inspector chart these are the colored T1 ↑ … T2 ↓ … waves, alternating bullish/bearish. The window auto-sizes per symbol so relevant swings aren't cropped.

7 · Fibonacci Levels

Fibonacci levels turn a recent price range into a ladder of support / resistance lines. They're anchored to the swing high and swing low of a ~90-day window (FIB_LOOKBACK_PERIOD) — shorter than the ~2y pattern window, so they track the current range. 0% is the high, 100% is the low; a "61.8% retracement" has given back 61.8% of the up-move.

LevelNameWhat it means
0%HighTop of the range — overhead resistance / the line a full recovery must reclaim.
23.6%Retracement (shallow)A minor pullback; strong trends often hold above it.
38.2%RetracementA healthy, moderate pullback; a common bounce zone in an uptrend.
50%Center LineRange midpoint — the bull/bear baseline (above constructive, below cautious). Drives the Technical Stance below.
61.8%Golden PocketThe most-watched level; holding it keeps the up-move intact, losing it opens the path to the base.
78.6%Retracement (deep)The last line before a full give-back.
100%BaseBottom of the range — the floor / support.

On the chart the lines are color-coded: 0% High (purple), 38.2% Fib (blue), 50% Center (amber), 61.8% Golden (red), 100% Base (grey). The Nearest Fib column shows the closest level, its kind, and price (e.g. 61.8% · Golden Pocket · $158.38) — the closer price sits, the more likely a retest/breakout.

8 · Technical Stance

A quick read of where price sits versus its Fibonacci structure (hover the cell for the trigger):

StanceTrigger
StrongPrice at/above the 50% center Fib level (bullish baseline).
AlertWithin 2% of the nearest Fib level — imminent retest/breakout.
CautiousBelow the 50% center level — wait for stabilization.
NeutralNo center level available; monitor key boundaries.
UnknownNot enough technical data.

9 · OBV and OBV vs Price

OBV (On-Balance Volume) adds volume on up-days and subtracts it on down-days. It is a flow gauge: rising OBV suggests accumulation, falling OBV suggests distribution.

FieldWhat it means
OBV TrendDirection and strength of OBV slope over the recent window (e.g. Accumulation, Distribution). This is OBV by itself.
OBV vs PriceFour-quadrant confirmation/divergence check between price direction and OBV direction.
OBV vs Price stateInterpretation
Bull confirm (✓)Price and OBV are both rising — move is volume-confirmed.
Bear confirm (✓)Price and OBV are both falling — down move is volume-confirmed.
Bearish divergence (⚠)Price rises while OBV weakens — upside may be fragile.
Bullish divergence (⚡)Price falls while OBV improves — downside may be fading.
Neutral (·)No clear signal or insufficient separation.

Use OBV as a confirmation layer, not a standalone trigger: strongest conviction usually comes when pattern/fib structure and OBV agree.

Agent Signal Record

After each assessment run, agent signals are captured as forward bets: SAI action, chart patterns, and confluence bias. Each bet waits the evaluation horizon (default 21 days), then is scored against a ±2% win/loss band (configurable via TRACK_RECORD_BAND_PCT).

Hit rates accumulate across all matured bets — there is no rolling window. Scores are read-only and do not yet change future runs.

Scoring rules (±2% band)

SignalWinLossNeutral
BullishPrice rises more than +2%Price falls more than −2%Move inside ±2%
BearishPrice falls more than −2%Price rises more than +2%Move inside ±2%
Hold / watchAlways neutral — move is stored but not win/loss

Hit rate = wins ÷ (wins + losses). Neutrals are shown as n in the W–L column.
